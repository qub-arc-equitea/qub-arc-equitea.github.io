Our Ground Rules

We follow the AAS Code of Ethics in our discussions, with our own guidance below. Anyone who does not adhere to this code of conduct will be barred from future meetings.

  1. This is a space for everyone to learn and grow.
    • Remember, no-one is perfect, If you make a mistake, remember to apologise and learn from it. If someone says something that is incorrect, please make sure to correct them kindly, rather than berating them.
  2. The voices and needs of the most vulnerable in any given conversation should be prioritised.
    • As such, please be conscious of how much space you’re taking up in a conversation. Please be extra conscious if you hold a privileged or dominant identity when discussing a form of inequality or oppression. Listen to and amplify the voices of those who have the lived experience; do not talk over them.
  3. Disagree with ideas, not people.
    • Use "I" statements to speak from your personal perspective, rather than making generalizations.
  4. Avoid playing devil’s advocate.
    • Specifically, do not argue for contrarian positions for the sake of debate. Only argue for positions that you actually hold.
  5. Be respectful of the emotions people may display – don’t let it take away from the content of their speech.
    • Be aware that there can be a lot of emotional weight associated with lived experiences. Therefore, people may misspeak or become emotional when discussing an experience they have lived. Please be conscious that this is rooted in emotion and should not take away from the content that they are trying to share.

These points where inspired by similar codes of conduct from the University of Warwick Equitea and the University of Michigan Equitea.

About Equitea

No one person is responsible for all of science - we are the sum of all the diverse minds that contribute towards our knowledge in every field. Not all of us are treated equally however, with many still subjected to acts of discrimination which robs them of the the ability to exist in academic circles.

We here in the Astrophysics Research Centre want to commit to promoting a welcoming, shared environment for anyone to engage in freely. This equitea project aims to be our way of amplifying marginalised voices, allowing us to learn form and improve our academic community for the current and future generations. This is a place for both minority groups and allies alike - we only ask that you listen and (most importantly) learn!

Mission Statement

Our mission at Equitea is to champion and foster equality, diversity and inclusivity within ARC. In our sessions, we aim to create safe spaces that foster dialogue, understanding and empathy while encouraging participants to actively listen, learn and engage in meaningful conversations. We are dedicated to creating a department where every member is respected, valued and empowered to achieve their potential.
